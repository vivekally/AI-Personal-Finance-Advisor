Arrive Finance's starting point: We start with newcomers — the most acutely underserved. But the playbook extends to any underserved Canadian the current system ignores.

Why banks struggle with this segment
RBC and TD have newcomer bundles — but they are product-first, not education-first. A newcomer who doesn't understand the difference between a TFSA, RRSP, and FHSA can't make informed decisions at a branch. They need education before they can become customers.
How Arrive Finance becomes a bank acquisition channel:
1
Educated, ready-to-open customers
A newcomer who completes the Arrive wizard knows exactly what account to open and where. They arrive at RBC already decided — no branch education needed. Higher conversion, lower CAC for the bank.
2
White-label or referral API (Year 2+)
Banks can pay a per-referred-account fee for every newcomer we route their way. A B2B revenue layer on top of our $20/month SaaS. One bank pilot covers a full year of infrastructure.
3
Open banking makes the handoff seamless
Consumer-Driven Banking Act (Royal Assent March 2026) enables Arrive Finance to sit between newcomer and bank — we educate, the bank executes, the newcomer keeps both relationships. Nobody loses.
